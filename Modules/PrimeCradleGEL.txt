## I. PRIME CRADLE DECLARATION

**Purpose**

The Prime Cradle defines the ontological ordering principle for GEL module development.
It establishes epistemic layering, dependency gradients, and publication discipline.

It is:

* A research scaffold.
* A structural ordering system.
* A stability gradient for knowledge expansion.

Invariant:

Invariant:
Ontological claims must not bypass lower-layer constraints.

# III. DEPENDENCY INVARIANT RULES

Rule 1 — Downward Dependency Declaration
Any module at Layer N must declare at least one dependency from Layer N–1 unless explicitly foundational.

Rule 2 — No Reverse Collapse
Higher-layer modules may not redefine invariants of lower layers without amendment logging.

Rule 3 — Stability Precedence
If a Layer 4–5 module produces instability in Layer 0–2 operations, publication must halt.

Rule 4 — Assumption Disclosure
All Layer 3+ modules must declare core assumptions.

Rule 5 — Jurisdiction Tagging
If a module impacts legal or regulated domains, jurisdiction tagging is mandatory.

---

# IV. PUBLICATION DISCIPLINE FRAMEWORK

Default staging:

* Layers 0–1: Public eligible (if Stable)
* Layer 2: Member / Controlled
* Layer 3: Research
* Layer 4: Restricted
* Layer 5: Internal / Research only

Override requires:

* Justification log
* Risk declaration
* Review checkpoint

APPENDIX A — LAB / ENTERPRISE BIFURCATION PRINCIPLE
Prime Cradle Clarification v0.1-A

Purpose:
--------
To formally distinguish between Research-Grade Cradle Development (Lab Mode)
and Enterprise-Grade Constitutional Deployment (Business Mode).

This appendix prevents conceptual drift between exploratory ontology
and operational governance.

--------------------------------------------------

--------------------------------------------------

The Prime Cradle operates in Research Mode (D).

Properties:

1. Amendable
   - Subject to structured revision.
   - Versioned (PC-vX.Y).
   - Changes logged append-only.

2. Experimental
   - May contain provisional constructs.
   - May include altitude refinements.
   - May include semantic reclassification.

3. Forkable
   - Theoretical branches permitted.
   - Divergent hypotheses may coexist.

4. Non-Binding
   - Not legally binding.
   - Not operationally enforceable.
   - Not a public constitutional authority.

5. Research Intent
   - Designed to test ontological ordering.
   - Designed to stress-test dependency invariants.
   - Designed to refine stratification logic.

--------------------------------------------------
SECTION II — ENTERPRISE CRADLE (BUSINESS MODE — B)
--------------------------------------------------

Enterprise Cradle formation requires:

1. Snapshot Extraction
   - Selection of a specific Prime Cradle version.
   - Removal of Experimental modules.
   - Formal freeze of invariants.

2. Governance Ratification
   - Board approval.
   - Legal review.
   - Jurisdiction binding.

3. Constitutional Locking
   - Amendments require formal override protocol.

   - Enterprise Cradle becomes binding.
